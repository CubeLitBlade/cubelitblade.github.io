Microsoft has stopped support for Internet Explorer

On June 15, 2022, Microsoft stopped supporting all versions of Internet Explorer. This means that after this date, unless you need to access some excessively old pages, Internet Explorer is not recommended under any circumstances.

We have to admit that Internet Explorer has been around for almost 30 years, from the beginning to the boom of the Internet, and at one time it had a huge share of the market with its unique advantages. However, today's Internet Explorer lags far behind other modern browsers in its support for modern web standards and technologies, causing developers to spend more time and effort on compatibility issues, which affects development efficiency and the user experience. Furthermore, Internet Explorer has multiple security vulnerabilities and issues that could be exploited by malicious attackers to conduct network attacks. In addition, Internet Explorer has poor performance compared to modern browsers in terms of loading speed, rendering speed, and JavaScript execution speed. By all accounts, it's time to say goodbye to the old guy.


Why am I seeing this page?

We note that you are accessing this page using Internet Explorer, or "Internet Explorer Mode" (also known as "Compatibility Mode") on some modern browsers. Internet Explorer is no longer supported on our website, so we are informing you of this.


What should I do?

If you are using Internet Explorer, please change your browser. If you are not using Internet Explorer and are seeing this page, try closing your browser's "Internet Explorer Mode" or "Compatibility Mode" and reloading the page you requested.
